Counter-Strike: Global Offensive

Counter-Strike: Global Offensive, commonly abbreviated to CS:GO, is an online first-person shooter (FPS). The video game was developed by Valve Corporation and their partner Hidden Path Entertainment.

Release

Counter-Strike: Global Offensive was released on August 21, 2012 for the PlayStation 3, Xbox 360, Microsoft Windows, and eventually Linux. Since its release, CS:GO has sold more than 17 million copies on the PC alone making it the 2nd most sold PC game of all time.

Popularity

Recently in the passed few years CS:GO has gained lots traction within common gamming community and the Major League Gaming (MLG) community where the game is played in professional competition. It was announced the week of February 22nd, 2016 that the next CS:GO competition, MLG Columbus 2016, as well as each CS:GO Major Championship prize pool has been increased to $1,000,000. In the MLG Columbus 2016 event there are 16 total teams from all over the world competing for the $1,000,000 prize.

Gameplay

Counter-Strike: Global Offensive is an objective-based multiplayer first-person shooter. Each player joins either the Terrorist or Counter-Terrorist team and attempts to complete objectives or eliminate the enemy team (determined by the game mode selected). The game operates in short rounds that end when all players on one side are dead or a team's objective is completed. For most game modes, once a player dies, they must wait until the round ends to re-spawn.

Players purchase weapons and equipment at the beginning of every round with money awarded based on their performance. Completing objectives or killing enemies earns the player money while negative actions, like killing a teammate or hostage, takes money away from the player. In addition, when a round ends all players receive some amount of money, with players on the winning team receiving substantially more.

Through later updates, the weapons within the game have cosmetic finishes, more commonly know as skins (camos), that cover the weapon. These skins can be achieved through the purchase of cases and keys, acquired from the Steam Marketplace or randomly as drops after completing game, to unlock said cases through a lottery type system. Each case offers unique skins for certain guns as well as a very rare knife skin. These weapon and knife skins are very sought after, but can also be purchased individually on the Steam Marketplace for CS:GO. Some of the rarest skins are worth up to $400 USD.
